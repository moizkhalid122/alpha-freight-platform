Freight Insurance: What You Need to Know

A comprehensive guide to freight insurance coverage, understanding policies, and protecting your shipments. Essential reading for all stakeholders.

February 3, 2026 6 min read By Alpha Freight Team

Insurance protects both money and trust

Freight insurance helps protect shipments against loss or damage during transit. Clear coverage and the right documents reduce disputes and keep operations moving smoothly—something Alpha Freight encourages for both suppliers and carriers.

Quick note: This is general guidance. Always review your policy wording and consult your insurer/broker for specifics.

1) Cargo insurance vs. liability: know the difference

These terms are often mixed up, but they are not the same.

  • Cargo insurance: covers the value of goods (subject to policy limits and exclusions)
  • Carrier liability: legal responsibility limits that may be lower than shipment value
  • Coverage depends on mode, contract terms, and policy wording

2) Understand coverage limits and exclusions

Most problems happen when expectations don’t match the policy.

  • Check per-shipment and per-vehicle limits
  • Review exclusions (theft, improper packaging, temperature deviation, etc.)
  • Confirm if high-value or sensitive cargo needs special endorsement

3) Document everything (before, during, after)

Good documentation speeds claims and reduces disputes.

  • Photos at pickup and delivery (seal, condition, pallets)
  • Clear notes on the POD (damage, shortage, exceptions)
  • Time-stamped communications and reference numbers

4) Claims basics: act fast and keep evidence

Claim timelines can be strict—don’t wait.

  • Notify the relevant parties immediately
  • Preserve packaging and damaged goods where possible
  • Keep invoices, packing lists, and delivery records ready

5) Best practices for suppliers and carriers

A few habits reduce risk dramatically.

  • Suppliers: declare special handling and cargo value upfront
  • Carriers: confirm coverage matches cargo type and route requirements
  • Everyone: use clear pickup/delivery instructions and avoid rushed handoffs
