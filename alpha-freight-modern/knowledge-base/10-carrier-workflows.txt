Alpha Freight Carrier Workflows

Available Loads page (Carrier)
The Available Loads page is your load board. Your goal is to quickly filter, pick the best loads, and avoid deadhead and timing traps.

How to use Available Loads step by step
1) Set filters first: pickup area, delivery area, equipment type, pickup date/time, and your max deadhead.
2) Scan the list for fit: pickup → delivery, equipment, pickup/delivery windows, weight/notes, and the posted rate.
3) Quick risk check before opening: appointment windows, commodity risk, detention/layover risk, and whether delivery location has reload potential.
4) Open the load for full details: exact addresses, notes, special requirements, and contact instructions.
5) Take action: View Load for details, Bid Now to submit your rate, Save to compare later.

What “good” looks like for a carrier
- Deadhead to pickup is controlled (set your own threshold).
- Equipment match is perfect (dry van vs reefer vs flatbed).
- Pickup timing is realistic for your hours and current position.
- Delivery lane has reload potential (backhaul or strong market).

My Loads page (Carrier)
My Loads shows the loads you accepted/booked and their current status. It is the operational execution page, not a search page.

How to use My Loads step by step
1) Open a load and confirm appointment windows and addresses.
2) Keep status updated: booked/assigned → arrived at pickup → loaded → in transit → delivered.
3) Watch exceptions: delay risk, reschedules, missing documents, or accessorial needs.
4) After delivery, confirm delivery status and upload required documents if needed (POD).

Bidding guidance (Carrier)
When you bid, you are balancing rate, timing, and risk. A “high pay” load can be bad if deadhead or delays destroy your net.

Simple bid checklist
- Loaded miles and deadhead miles
- Fuel cost estimate (based on your MPG and diesel)
- Appointment windows (can you make them)
- Payment/payout timing (if relevant)

Tracking basics (Carrier)
Tracking is how you keep the shipment visible from pickup to delivery. The core flow is pickup confirmed → in transit updates → delay alerts → ETA updates → delivery confirmed.
