Frequently Asked Questions about Alpha Freight:

Q: How fast is the AI load matching?
A: Our AI analyzes thousands of data points to find the perfect carrier match in under 60 seconds, significantly reducing your planning time.

Q: How do you verify your carriers?
A: Every carrier in our network undergoes a rigorous 5-step vetting process, including insurance verification, safety rating checks, and past performance reviews.

Q: What is the 7-day payout guarantee?
A: We ensure carriers are paid within 7 days of delivery confirmation, helping maintain cash flow and a healthy partnership within our network.

Q: Is real-time tracking included?
A: Yes, every shipment booked through Alpha Freight includes full GPS tracking and automated status updates for complete supply chain visibility.

Q: What does the carrier Wallet section do?
A: The Wallet section helps carriers review available balance, payout activity, and transaction history connected to completed loads and withdrawals.

Q: What is shown in carrier Earnings?
A: The Earnings page is designed to show total earnings, active revenue, average revenue per load, completed shipment count, and revenue trends over time.

Q: What are Available Loads?
A: Available Loads are active shipment opportunities in the marketplace that carriers can review based on route, timing, equipment, and earning potential.

Q: What are Smart Loads?
A: Smart Loads are AI-recommended load opportunities intended to match carrier route fit, timing, and profitability more intelligently.

Q: What is the Driver Panel?
A: The Driver Panel lets carriers add and manage driver profiles, including contact details, license reference, experience, and operating region.

Q: What is the My Vehicles section?
A: My Vehicles is used to manage fleet units, registration details, compliance files, operating regions, and core vehicle records.

Q: How does Post a Load work for suppliers?
A: Suppliers enter pickup and delivery details, timing, cargo information, equipment needs, budget range, payment method, and special requirements before publishing a load.

Q: What is the difference between Pay Instant and Pay Later?
A: Pay Instant is for immediate payment processing, while Pay Later keeps eligible shipment payments in a queue until the supplier chooses to move them into instant checkout.

Q: How do supplier bids work?
A: Suppliers can review bids from carriers on posted loads, compare them, accept the best offer, and automatically move the load into booked status with the chosen carrier.

Q: What support options are available?
A: Alpha Freight support includes live chat, email support, phone support, and FAQ guidance for operational, account, shipment, and payment questions.

Q: How is load pricing decided?
A: Pricing is mainly based on route distance, pickup and delivery timing, cargo weight and volume, equipment type, and any special handling required. Longer routes, tighter schedules, heavier cargo, and specialist equipment usually increase the rate.

Q: How should suppliers think about minimum and maximum budget?
A: The minimum budget should reflect the lowest realistic amount for the route and service level, while the maximum budget gives room for better carrier quality, urgent movement, or specialist handling. A narrow budget may reduce match quality, while a balanced range helps secure better-fit carriers faster.

Q: How much does special handling affect pricing?
A: Special handling can increase the shipment rate depending on complexity. Refrigerated loads, ADR/hazardous movement, tail lift support, and white glove handling all require extra operational planning, equipment, or compliance, so they usually raise the final price.

Q: What pricing impact can urgent, refrigerated, ADR, tail lift, and white glove loads have?
A: Urgent loads can increase price because of tighter timing and priority scheduling. Refrigerated loads require temperature-controlled equipment. ADR loads require certified compliance and higher-risk handling. Tail lift jobs require specific loading capability. White glove jobs need premium handling and extra care. These requirements can each push pricing above a standard load rate.

Q: What are typical support response times?
A: Live chat is intended for the fastest response and is best for active shipment questions. Email support is better for non-urgent operational or account issues. Phone support is available when the issue needs direct coordination. Urgent shipment cases are prioritised ahead of general queries.

Q: When does support escalation happen?
A: Support escalation happens when an issue affects live shipment execution, missed pickup or delivery risk, tracking failure, payment delay, account access problems, or unresolved complaints that need manager-level review.

Q: How are urgent shipment issues handled?
A: Urgent shipment cases are treated as priority support. The case should include shipment reference, route, current status, and the exact issue. Support then coordinates the next action, such as live follow-up, route clarification, or operational escalation.

Q: How are complaints handled?
A: Complaints are logged, reviewed, and assigned for follow-up. Alpha Freight aims to gather shipment context, communication history, and the operational impact before deciding the next step. More serious complaints can be escalated for deeper review.

Q: Is there a refund or dispute policy?
A: If there is a payment or service dispute, the case should be reviewed with shipment records, timeline details, and communication history. The platform is designed to support dispute review in a controlled way rather than issuing automatic refunds without verification.

Q: What are the five main carrier vetting steps?
A: The standard Alpha Freight vetting flow can be understood as: 1) identity and business registration review, 2) insurance verification, 3) safety and compliance checks, 4) document and equipment review, and 5) performance and reliability assessment.

Q: What is checked during insurance verification?
A: Insurance review is intended to confirm active commercial cover, validity dates, and whether the carrier is properly protected for the type of transport work being performed.

Q: What is checked in a safety rating review?
A: Safety review is meant to look at operating reliability, compliance record, incident history where relevant, and whether the carrier meets expected standards for trustworthy shipment execution.

Q: What compliance documents are usually required from carriers?
A: Typical compliance records can include business registration details, insurance proof, operating authority information where needed, driver or fleet-related compliance records, and equipment-linked documentation for specialist jobs.

Q: When does re-verification happen?
A: Re-verification should happen when key documents expire, insurance needs renewal, compliance status changes, safety concerns are raised, or periodic account review is triggered.

Q: When does tracking start?
A: Tracking effectively begins once a shipment is booked and prepared for execution. Visibility becomes more meaningful from pickup readiness onward.

Q: What update is shown at pickup?
A: Pickup updates should confirm that the load is accepted into live execution, with shipment status moving from planning or booked state into operational progress.

Q: What statuses can appear during transit?
A: Common in-transit style updates can include booked, pickup scheduled, picked up, in transit, delayed, arrival expected, and delivered or completed depending on the shipment stage.

Q: What happens after delivery?
A: Once delivery is confirmed, the shipment can move into completed status, tracking closes operationally, and downstream steps like payout processing or shipment history review can continue.

Q: What happens if a load is delayed?
A: A delay should trigger a status update and support context where needed. The goal is to keep both sides informed with revised timing and operational visibility rather than leaving the shipment without communication.
