5. Fast 7-Day Payouts
Guaranteed quick payments for carriers to maintain a healthy and motivated network.

Expanded operational flow:
- Suppliers begin by entering load details such as origin, destination, schedule, cargo information, equipment needs, and pricing range
- Carriers review available opportunities, submit bids, or move on high-fit loads based on route and earning potential
- Once a carrier is selected, the shipment moves into booked execution and both sides gain better visibility
- Shipment progress can move through active, booked, in-transit, and completed stages depending on the workflow
- Tracking and support can work together when shipment-specific help is needed

Verification and safety flow:
- Carrier matching is intended to prioritize verified and reliable transport partners
- Verification is tied to trust, insurance review, safety checks, and operating confidence
- Secure operations are designed to reduce risk and improve shipment quality across the platform

Pricing and payment flow:
- Suppliers can review pricing inputs before publishing a load
- Shipment pricing can be affected by route, timing, equipment, cargo requirements, and special handling
- Suppliers can use Pay Instant or Pay Later style payment flows where supported by the workflow
- Carriers follow payout flow after delivery confirmation, with a target seven-day payout window

Detailed supplier journey:
- Suppliers create an account and verify key business details so carriers can trust the load from day one
- Supplier approval is typically completed within 1-2 business days, subject to verification checks
- When posting a load, suppliers should include pickup location, delivery location, cargo type, dimensions, weight, volume, delivery date or time, and any special instructions
- Clear load details reduce back-and-forth, improve bid quality, and reduce delivery delays
- Suppliers can compare multiple bids, review carrier ratings, check vehicle specifications, and review delivery history before choosing a carrier
- After selecting the winning bid, suppliers confirm the carrier, set up secure Stripe payment, receive confirmation details, and track the shipment in real time
- After delivery, suppliers can confirm completion, trigger payment release, rate the carrier experience, and download invoices or receipts

Detailed carrier journey:
- Carriers register and complete verification to improve trust and qualify for better loads
- Carrier approval is typically completed within 1-2 business days, subject to verification of company details, vehicle registration, insurance, and license checks
- Carriers should maintain accurate vehicle profiles, including vehicle type, specifications, capacity, dimensions, registration documents, and insurance details
- Available loads can be filtered by location, route, load requirements, delivery deadlines, and supplier ratings
- When submitting bids, carriers should clearly provide price, available dates, and any special notes or terms
- Once selected, carriers accept the assignment, confirm pickup details, update delivery status, and complete delivery confirmation
- After delivery confirmation, carriers can track payment history and download payment receipts from the platform

Stripe payment operations:
- Payments are processed through Stripe for secure payment handling
- When a supplier selects a carrier bid, payment is securely held by Stripe until delivery is confirmed
- Once delivery is confirmed and completion is verified, payment is automatically released to the carrier account within 24-48 hours
- Both sides benefit from payment protection because funds are secured during the active job
- Stripe accepts major credit cards, debit cards, bank transfers, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and direct debit where applicable
- A transparent brokerage fee is typically around 5-10 percent of the load value and should be shown before a bid is accepted
- Stripe processing fees follow standard industry rates

Tracking and status visibility:
- Platform tracking is designed to support progress updates from pickup through delivery confirmation
- Suppliers should expect real-time GPS tracking, live status updates, and delivery confirmation visibility where available
- Carriers are expected to update shipment status during active execution to keep suppliers informed
- Better tracking helps both sides reduce surprises, make faster decisions, and maintain proof of service

Security and trust protections:
- All platform traffic is protected with 256-bit SSL encryption
- User and payment data are protected through secure systems and encrypted storage practices
- Stripe is PCI DSS Level 1 certified, and card details are not stored on Alpha Freight servers
- Platform security may include fraud detection, secure APIs, regular audits, and optional two-factor authentication
- Alpha Freight follows GDPR-aligned and international data protection practices
- Supplier and carrier accounts are verified before approval through checks such as business registration, licenses, insurance, and contact information

Disputes and mediation:
- If a dispute happens, Alpha Freight support provides mediation to help both sides reach a fair outcome
- Payment remains securely held during an active dispute until the issue is resolved
- Alpha Freight provides mediation support only and is not a direct party to the transport contract between supplier and carrier
