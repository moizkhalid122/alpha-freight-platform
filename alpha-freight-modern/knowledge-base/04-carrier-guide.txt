Carrier operational workflow:
- Open Available Loads to review live opportunities in the marketplace
- Use route, equipment, timing, and payout logic to evaluate the best fit
- Submit a bid or book a matching load
- Once assigned, manage the shipment through My Loads
- Use tracking and status updates during execution
- After delivery confirmation, payout processing begins
- Monitor financial performance through Wallet and Earnings

Carrier payout and earnings details:
- Alpha Freight aims to process carrier payouts within 7 days after delivery confirmation
- Wallet shows available balance and payout activity
- Earnings gives performance analytics such as total revenue, average per load, active revenue, and completed jobs
- Instant payout can be offered for a small fee where supported by the workflow

Carrier verification standards:
- Carrier onboarding is based on a 5-step vetting flow:
  1. Identity and business registration review
  2. Insurance verification
  3. Safety and compliance screening
  4. Fleet and document validation
  5. Performance and reliability review
- Insurance verification is intended to confirm active and suitable transport coverage
- Safety screening is used to reduce risk and improve shipment confidence
- Compliance review may include business, fleet, driver, and specialist equipment records
- Re-verification can happen when documents expire, insurance changes, compliance issues appear, or scheduled account review is due

Carrier pricing and rate logic:
- Load value is influenced by route distance, shipment timing, equipment fit, weight, volume, urgency, and handling complexity
- Higher-value loads are more likely when specialist equipment or premium execution is required
- Urgent loads can carry stronger rates because of tighter scheduling pressure
- Refrigerated work can increase rates because of temperature-controlled equipment needs
- ADR or hazardous movement can increase rates because of compliance and risk requirements
- Tail lift requirements can increase rates when specialist loading support is needed
- White glove handling can increase rates because of premium handling and extra care expectations

Carrier tracking and shipment execution:
- Tracking becomes active once the load is booked and the shipment moves into live operations
- Common shipment states can include booked, pickup scheduled, picked up, in transit, delayed, arrival expected, and completed
- Delay updates are intended to improve transparency for both the carrier and the supplier
- After delivery confirmation, the shipment closes operationally and payout processing can continue

Fleet and driver management:
- Vehicles can be registered with unit name, registration number, make, model, year, capacity, and operating region
- Compliance or registration files can be uploaded for fleet records
- Drivers can be added with full name, email, phone, license number, years of experience, and operating region
- Driver Panel is designed for operational readiness and dispatch coordination

Carrier support and help:
- Support includes live chat, email support, and phone support
- Common help topics include withdrawing earnings, Smart Loads, vehicle tracking, and payment security
- Premium carriers can receive 24/7 help according to the support interface
