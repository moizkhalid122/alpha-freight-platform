Supplier dashboard sections:
- Dashboard: Overview of shipment activity, load status, and business workflow
- AI Assistant: Get help with posting loads, carrier matching, pricing, tracking, and supplier workflow
- Post a Load: Create a shipment by adding route, cargo, timing, equipment, budget, and payment details
- My Posts: Review all posted loads by status such as active, booked, in-transit, or completed
- My Bids: Review carrier bids, compare offers, accept the best bid, or reject other bids

Supplier posting workflow:
- Start in Post a Load and add pickup and delivery locations
- Enter pickup and delivery timing, cargo type, weight, volume, value, and equipment
- Add special shipment requirements such as tail lift, refrigerated, ADR-certified, or white glove handling
- Review price range, payment method, and additional notes
- Publish the load so carriers can bid or be matched through the platform
- Monitor the load through My Posts and review carrier offers in My Bids
- Accept a bid to assign a carrier and move the load into booked status
- Track execution progress until delivery is completed

Pricing and payment details:
- Pricing is influenced by route, timing, cargo requirements, equipment type, and special handling
- Suppliers can set minimum and maximum budget ranges when posting a load
- A suggested price can be estimated from route distance and shipment data
- Payment routes include Pay Instant and Pay Later
- Pay Instant supports card payment workflow for shipment orders
- Pay Later keeps orders in a queue until the supplier chooses to move them into instant checkout

Supplier pricing guidance:
- Route distance is one of the main pricing drivers
- Tighter pickup or delivery windows can increase the final shipment rate
- Heavier, larger, or more complex cargo can raise pricing
- Equipment needs such as refrigerated trailers or specialist handling can increase the rate above a standard load
- Minimum budget should represent the lowest realistic offer for the route and service level
- Maximum budget gives room for better-fit carriers, urgent movement, or premium service quality
- A balanced budget range generally improves matching speed and carrier quality
- Urgent loads can cost more because they require fast response and tighter scheduling
- Refrigerated loads can cost more due to temperature-control requirements
- ADR loads can cost more because of compliance and risk-sensitive handling
- Tail lift loads can cost more where specific loading capability is needed
- White glove loads can cost more because of extra care, premium handling, and added service expectation

Bids and shipment control:
- Suppliers can receive bids from multiple carriers on the same load
- Accepting one bid can reject the other bids on that load
- When a bid is accepted, the load is updated to booked and linked to the selected carrier
- My Posts can also show progress stages such as active, booked, in-transit, and completed

Tracking and carrier confidence:
- Real-time tracking is intended to provide shipment visibility from pickup to delivery
- Verified carriers help improve trust, reliability, and shipment confidence
- Alpha Freight focuses on fast carrier matching, verified network quality, and shipment transparency

Supplier tracking expectations:
- Tracking becomes meaningful once a load is booked and prepared for live execution
- Pickup updates should indicate the start of operational movement
- In-transit tracking should show progress, status visibility, and any delay context where relevant
- Delivery confirmation should close the operational shipment stage and move the load into completed history
- Delay visibility is intended to keep suppliers informed instead of leaving the shipment unclear

Support and dispute handling:
- Live chat is the fastest support path for active shipment issues
- Email support is better for non-urgent account or operational questions
- Phone support can be used when direct coordination is needed
- Urgent shipment problems should be escalated when timing, delivery, or execution is at risk
- Complaint handling should include shipment context, timing details, and communication history
- Disputes should be reviewed with shipment records and operational evidence before any final resolution is made

Supplier support and help:
- Support includes live chat, email support, and phone support
- Shipment-linked support can include route and status context
- Support is designed to help with posting, tracking, pricing, and operational questions
